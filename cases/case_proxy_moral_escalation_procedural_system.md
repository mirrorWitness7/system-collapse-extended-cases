# Case: Proxy Moral Escalation in Procedural Systems (Anonymized)

> Structural focus: Incentive Misalignment, Narrative Inflation, Asymmetric Risk Transfer

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This case describes how a **narrow procedural issue** escalated into a prolonged conflict due to proxy advocacy.

A secondary actor reframed a solvable procedural matter into a moral confrontation while bearing none of the downside risk, destabilizing outcomes for the primary actor.

---

## 2. Actor Roles (Structural)

### Primary Actor
- Directly affected by the decision
- Requires limited, pragmatic resolution
- Bears financial, reputational, and time costs

### Proxy Advocate
- Not directly affected
- High ideological certainty
- Low personal exposure
- High narrative confidence

This creates **asymmetric risk ownership**.

---

## 3. System Context

The system operates on:
- Logs
- Records
- Procedural determinism

Narrative intent and moral framing are secondary to documented evidence.

---

## 4. Core Failure Mode

The proxy reframes:
- Procedural violation → moral injustice
- Narrow dispute → systemic indictment
- Resolution → confrontation

This inflates scope without improving success probability.

---

## 5. Strategic Errors Introduced

### A. Case Contamination
- Unrelated grievances imported
- Orthogonal domains merged
- Evidence burden increases

### B. Scope Inflation
- Escalation replaces remediation
- Costs rise nonlinearly
- System defensiveness increases

### C. Risk Misallocation
- Proxy bears no downside
- Primary actor absorbs all consequences

---

## 6. Why Escalation Persists

- Moral certainty substitutes for probabilistic reasoning
- Confidence replaces evidence
- External validation reinforces belief loops

This creates epistemic dominance without control.

---

## 7. Likely Resolution Pattern

- System requests records
- Narrative collapses under logs
- Case exits quietly
- Relationship damage remains

The system survives; trust does not.

---

## 8. Archetype Mapping

- Proxy Warrior
- Moral Escalation Loop
- Asymmetric Risk Advocacy
